My local matters.

The places you and I love and what we can do to protect them.

ALERT! Right now the government wants to change the why and the how of the Conservation Act. At present we, as the public, through conservation boards get a say in how our public lands are used. The proposed changes not only open public lands to ‘disposal’ (for commercial use) but also places that sometimes irreversible decision in the hands of one person — a politician who reflects the thinking of the day. Our forests, mountains and rivers deserve a more thoughtful approach.

Conservation Land is OUR land

60% of our conservation land is at risk of being sold off or leased. This government is putting (foreign) companies and short-term payouts over our people and places.

Protect New Zealand's Conservation Land by writing to Parliament and your local MP. Simply click on the link. It only takes 5 minutes.

It doesn't matter who you vote for, or where you come from, our Conservation Land is part of who I am, you are — we are.

This map shows which areas are at-risk, which MPs represent them, and how those MPs have voted on the environment so that you can write to them and let them know this is not okay.
